### How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?

Interpreting user needs starts with understanding the goals, constraints, and preferences of the user. Creating user
stories helps by breaking these needs into actionable, small tasks that describe a specific feature from the user’s
perspective. This ensures that the development process remains user-centered, and it makes prioritizing and planning
features easier.

### How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

I approach development iteratively, starting with small, testable pieces of functionality. Agile practices such as daily
standups, sprint planning, and retrospectives help me maintain focus, adapt to changes quickly, and continuously improve
both code quality and team collaboration. In future projects, I hope to continue using these Agile practices to maintain
responsiveness to project requirements and stakeholder feedback.

### What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

Being a good team member means communicating clearly, collaborating effectively, respecting others’ ideas, and
contributing reliably to the team’s goals. It also involves actively participating in planning and review sessions,
providing constructive feedback, and being willing to help others when needed. Good team members balance their individual
responsibilities with the success of the team as a whole.
